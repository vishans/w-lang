Getting started
workout language has been designed to be an efficient and simple language. Let's not forget, the goal is fast and efficient journaling and storage of workouts.

A typical workout script consists of clauses. workout language has 3 legal clauses.
  • meta:
  • workout:
  • set:

A script should have a single meta: clause and a single workout: clause.
It can have one to many set: clauses.

The clauses should also appear in that order. You always start with a meta: clause,
followed by a workout: clause,
and then followed by one or many set: clauses.

Everything that is indented inside a clause belongs to it. If you're familiar with the Python Programming Language, the clause structure has been inspired from Python's class/function definition.

Essentially, that was the basic of the structures in workout language. Before we finish here, below is an example of a workout script. Don't worry about everything in there; just look at the clauses and how the are laid out. 
    meta:
        debug
        csv

    workout:
        61kg 60.6kg 
        18:00 
        45min

    set:
        "squat" 1-15 25kg type="warm up"
        . 10 30kg

        "REST" 3min

        . 0-25 50kg type="hypertrophy"
        . 5 45kg

        "REST" 3min45s

    set:
        "Running" type="cardio" 25min15s
        "REST" 5min

Alright! That's it for the basic structures.
